Clinical Guidelines

AHA / ACC Standards
01

vital_signs Blood Pressure Management

  • Target resting BP should be below 130/80 mmHg for adults at increased cardiovascular risk.
  • Hypertension Stage 1 (130–139/80–89 mmHg) requires lifestyle modifications and potential pharmacotherapy.
  • Hypertension Stage 2 (≥ 140/90 mmHg) warrants immediate dual-drug antihypertensive therapy.
  • Home BP monitoring is recommended for accurate assessment — clinic readings can be 5–10 mmHg higher.
02

biotech Cholesterol & Lipid Profile

  • Desirable total cholesterol: < 200 mg/dL. Borderline high: 200–239 mg/dL. High: ≥ 240 mg/dL.
  • LDL-C should be the primary target — optimal level is < 100 mg/dL for high-risk patients.
  • Statin therapy recommended for patients with 10-year ASCVD risk ≥ 7.5% and LDL-C ≥ 70 mg/dL.
  • Fasting lipid panel should be repeated every 4–6 years for adults aged 20+ with normal levels.
03

ecg_heart ECG Interpretation & ST Segment

  • ST depression ≥ 1 mm during exercise is considered a positive test for myocardial ischemia.
  • Downsloping ST depression carries the highest risk — more significant than horizontal or upsloping patterns.
  • Exercise stress testing should achieve ≥ 85% of age-predicted maximum heart rate (220 – age).
  • A 12-lead ECG should be the first-line investigation for all patients presenting with chest pain.
04

water_drop Blood Sugar & Metabolic Health

  • Fasting blood sugar > 120 mg/dL is a significant risk factor and may indicate pre-diabetes or diabetes.
  • Diabetes doubles the risk of cardiovascular disease and is considered a "coronary risk equivalent."
  • HbA1c target of < 7% is recommended for most adults with diabetes to reduce microvascular risk.
  • Metabolic syndrome (≥3 of: high BP, high glucose, high triglycerides, low HDL, large waist) dramatically increases CVD risk.
05

directions_run Exercise & Lifestyle Modifications

  • At least 150 minutes/week of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous activity.
  • Exercise-induced angina should be evaluated with graded stress testing before clearing for physical activity.
  • Smoking cessation reduces CVD risk by 50% within 1 year and to near-normal levels within 5–15 years.
  • Mediterranean-style diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and omega-3 fatty acids is cardioprotective.
06

emergency When to Seek Emergency Care

  • Chest pain lasting > 15 minutes unrelieved by rest or nitroglycerin — call emergency services immediately.
  • Sudden onset of shortness of breath, cold sweat, nausea, or lightheadedness accompanying chest discomfort.
  • Pain radiating to left arm, jaw, neck, or back — classic signs of acute myocardial infarction.
  • Time is critical: achieving reperfusion within 90 minutes (door-to-balloon time) significantly improves outcomes.
